**Entry-level jobs in quantum computing** exist but are competitive and often require strong fundamentals in physics, computer science, math (especially linear algebra), and programming—particularly Python. Many roles are accessible with a bachelor's degree (or nearing completion), though a master's helps, and PhDs dominate advanced research positions. Pure "quantum" roles for fresh graduates are rarer than in classical tech, but the talent shortage creates opportunities, especially via internships that frequently convert to full-time offers.

### Common Entry-Level and Early-Career Roles
Here are typical starting positions:

- **Quantum Intern / Research Intern** (most common entry point)  
  Roles at companies like IBM, Google Quantum AI, NVIDIA, Quantinuum, Rigetti, IonQ, PsiQuantum, and national labs (e.g., Fermilab). Tasks include running quantum circuits on simulators/hardware, contributing to open-source projects (Qiskit, Cirq), data analysis, or assisting with experiments. Many are paid summer/fall programs (e.g., 10–12 weeks).

- **Junior Quantum Software Developer / Engineer**  
  Writing and testing quantum algorithms, developing applications using SDKs, or building hybrid quantum-classical tools.

- **Research Assistant / Associate**  
  Often in universities, national labs, or company R&D. Involves simulations, literature reviews, and supporting experiments.

- **Quantum Hardware Support / Lab Technician / Operations Roles**  
  Assisting with cryogenic systems, microwave control, or device characterization. More engineering-focused and sometimes more accessible with a bachelor's in EE, physics, or related fields.

- **Quantum Applications / Algorithm Developer (Junior)**  
  Focusing on specific domains like optimization, chemistry simulation, or machine learning.

- **Adjacent/Support Roles** (easier entry without deep quantum experience):  
  Technical writing, quality assurance/testing, software engineering in quantum-adjacent teams, business development, or classical infrastructure supporting quantum systems.

Other titles: Quantum Calibrations Intern, Quantum Error Correction Intern, Project Engineering Intern.

### Salary Expectations (US, as of 2026)
- **Internships**: Often $20–$45/hour or stipends (varies by location/company).
- **Full-time entry-level**: $70,000–$120,000 base (higher in high-cost areas like SF/NY; total comp can be more with equity/benefits). Mid-level quickly rises to $120k+.

### Requirements and Qualifications
- **Education**: Bachelor's in Physics, Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Mathematics, or related. Master's preferred for many technical roles. No PhD required for most entry-level.
- **Key Skills**:
  - Strong Python proficiency.
  - Quantum SDK experience (Qiskit, Cirq, PennyLane, Braket, Q#).
  - Linear algebra, probability, basic quantum mechanics (superposition, entanglement, gates).
  - Git, Jupyter Notebooks.
  - Problem-solving and documentation skills.
- **Nice-to-Haves**: Personal projects (e.g., GitHub quantum algorithms), hackathons, online courses (IBM Quantum, Coursera, edX), or contributions to open-source.

**Note**: You don't always need a physics degree—strong programmers or engineers with demonstrated quantum interest can enter, especially in software or support roles.

### How to Break In
1. **Build a portfolio** — Implement quantum algorithms, simulate circuits, or contribute to Qiskit/Cirq.
2. **Apply aggressively to internships** — These are the primary gateway.
3. **Network** — Use LinkedIn, Quantum Jobs List, quantum conferences, university programs (e.g., Chicago Quantum Exchange).
4. **Target employers** — Big tech (IBM, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, NVIDIA), pure-play (Quantinuum, IonQ, Rigetti, PsiQuantum), startups, national labs, and government initiatives.
5. **Job boards** — QuantumJobs.us, QuantumJobsList.com, Indeed, LinkedIn (filter for entry-level/intern), company career pages.

### Outlook
The field is growing, but entry-level pure quantum roles remain limited compared to broader tech. Hybrid skills (quantum + classical computing, domain expertise like chemistry/finance) are increasingly valued. Internships and demonstrated initiative are the most reliable paths for new graduates or career switchers.

Start with free resources like IBM Quantum Learning or Qiskit textbooks, build projects, and apply broadly. The shortage of qualified talent means motivated candidates with the right preparation have a real shot.
