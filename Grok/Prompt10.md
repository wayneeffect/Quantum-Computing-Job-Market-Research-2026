**The quantum computing market is currently worth roughly $1.5–3.5 billion (as of 2025–mid-2026)** and is experiencing rapid growth, though estimates vary by source due to differences in scope (hardware, software, services, cloud access).

### Current Market Size (2025–2026)
- **2025 estimates**: Commonly around **$1.4–1.5 billion** (e.g., Precedence Research: $1.44B; Fortune Business Insights: $1.53B; similar figures from SNS Insider and others).
- Some analysts (e.g., MarketsandMarkets) report higher figures, around **$3.5 billion in 2025**, likely including broader services and adoption.
- The market has crossed into the low multibillion-dollar range, with over 300 companies actively adopting or experimenting, per McKinsey’s 2026 Quantum Technology Monitor.

### Growth Projections
Projections show strong compound annual growth rates (CAGRs) of **20–42%**, driven by hardware advances, cloud access (QaaS), error correction progress, and applications in optimization, simulation, chemistry, finance, and ML.

| Timeframe       | Projected Market Size                  | CAGR (approx.)          | Notes / Source Examples |
|-----------------|----------------------------------------|-------------------------|-------------------------|
| **2030**       | $4–20 billion                         | 20–42%                 | MarketsandMarkets: $20.2B (from $3.52B in 2025, 41.8% CAGR); more conservative ~$4–7B in others. |
| **2035**       | $14–19+ billion (direct market)       | 29–32%                 | Precedence: ~$19.4B; Fortune: ~$18B range; SNS ~$19B. |
| **2040**       | Provider market: $90–170 billion      | 26–35%+                | BCG: Sustaining $90–170B for hardware/software providers. |

**Broader economic impact** (value creation for end-users, not just vendor revenue):
- **$1 trillion+ cumulative** by 2035 (The Quantum Insider).
- BCG: $450–850 billion by 2040.
- McKinsey: Potentially trillions in value across industries by the mid-2030s, with quantum computing as the largest segment of quantum technologies.

### Key Drivers and Context
- **Hardware, software, and services** (especially cloud-based) fuel growth. North America leads, followed by strong activity in Europe and Asia-Pacific.
- High CAGRs reflect the shift from research/NISQ-era systems to early commercial use cases and scaling efforts.
- Forecasts vary because the market includes hardware sales, quantum-as-a-service, professional services, and adjacent quantum tech. Economic impact (value unlocked for users) is much larger than direct vendor revenue.

**Bottom line**: The direct market is still early-stage (low billions today) but is on a steep growth trajectory, potentially reaching tens of billions by 2030–2035 and creating hundreds of billions to trillions in broader economic value. These are forward-looking estimates and could shift with breakthroughs in error correction or commercialization timelines. McKinsey describes 2026 as a potential “commercial tipping point.”

For the most precise figures, check recent reports from McKinsey, BCG, MarketsandMarkets, or Fortune Business Insights, as numbers are updated frequently.
